Extended About Me

My interests span from the scientific to the creative. Originally enrolled at the Community College of Allegheny County, (CCAC) for the undergraduate program for Psychology. I then moved into Software Development.

My inclination for psychology was always driven by my intuition and personal experience with mental health. With The scientific method as a framework to study the human brain and behaviors, psychology remains a deeply nestled affinity.

The creative side of mind was another important aspect that bubbled up and directed my sails into a new domain, notably apps.

The catalyst for diving into an Intro to Programming course spurred through my own mobile app idea. I held onto it for years and never thought I was the ‘type’ for programming, coding or engineering. However, with support from a dear friend, and some self-determination, I took on the challenge to learn to code.

I self-taught Java as my first language. I slowly came around to these novel, highly technical and abstract concepts. I then enrolled in courses at my college. Java 1 and 2, Intro to C, which expanded into Web Development, Databases, Networking and an Android Mobile App course.

I graduated with two AA (Associate of the Arts) degrees along with a certificate in Software Development. A participating member of my college Computer Club where I became more affluent with the culture of other software dev students and faculty. Learning, assisting and socializing with those in the space. I then branched out into local communities in Pittsburgh to network and continue in the presence of like minded people who share a career or interest for software, design or engineering. I was an attendee of Code and Coffee Pittsburgh and a regular attendee at Code and Supply in Pittsburgh.

Lastly, I was fortunate enough to intern at Carnegie Mellon's Robotic Academy, where I learned about the electro and mechanical foundations of robots, autonomous bots, and a great deal about AI and Machine Learning throughout the training. I find that my understanding of technology and design is ever evolving and it only helps to draw more connections to solving real world problems and being effective in many endeavors.
